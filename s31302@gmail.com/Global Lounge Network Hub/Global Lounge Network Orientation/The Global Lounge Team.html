The Global Lounge Community Animators are here to build community and support you to see your ideas through to fruition.  They are expert connectors and want to help you find collaboration points within the network and beyond.  Even though the tandem Coordinators don’t have Community Animator in their title, their work is based on the same model – student-to-student co-creation of our programs.

Moussa Niang, Global Lounge Community Animator

My name is Moussa and I am a fourth-year student in the Department of Political science at UBC.  I am originally from Dakar, Senegal but I have spent most of my life in Canada. I center most of my work on antiracism and social justice. Whether it is in my writing, coursework, extracurriculars, or podcast conversations, I constantly make it a crucial point to honor all the different intersections that we face as humans.

Through my passion for international relations, social justice and my past experiences as a Global Lounge Representative, I believe that the Global Lounge is an exceptional space for intercultural dialogue and personal development. The Global Lounge has been a crucial connection in the numerous executive positions I have had on the UBC campus (such as the Africa Awareness Initiative’s VP External) and it has always greatly contributed to the success of our projects.

My experience as the VP External for the UBC Africa Awareness Initiative was an important part of the work I do on campus. As a visible minority, Black/African students are left with heavy work that involves creating safe spaces where our shared and complex experiences are honored and respected. Work such as this, requires an immense amount of intentionality and perseverance and I am excited to apply these qualities into the work I do as a GLCA.

In addition to my position as a GLCA, I am currently Residence Advising in Exchange and an Outreach Coordinator for the 2021 Afrocentrism conference.
